Ownership & Stewardship — in plain language

The governance cockpit that answers "what accountability risk needs attention right now?" — not a spreadsheet of who owns what.

📄 For: Product owner review⏱ Read: ~4 min
The one-sentence version

Accountability, captured once, surfaced where it matters

Fabric can tell you who has permission. It cannot tell you who is accountable — who runs the workspace, who explains the metric, who signs off when the auditor calls.

This page captures the accountability layer, splits it cleanly between workspaces (operational) and semantic models (business logic), and sorts everything by risk so the gaps surface first.

Two layers, different responsibilities

Workspace owns operations · Model owns business logic

Same page, two tabs. Different role sets because the responsibilities differ — forcing identical roles where they don't fit just makes the UX vague.

🏢 Workspace Operations

Container · boundary · lifecycle · access

  • Workspace owner — accountable operationally (capacity, lifecycle, escalation)
  • Primary contact — optional day-to-day operational/escalation contact

No SME or Steward here — they're semantically weak on a container.

📊 Semantic model Business logic

KPIs · DAX · metric definitions · analytical truth

  • Owner — accountable for the model
  • SME — explains the metric / business logic / KPI
  • Steward — data quality + change review (can be several)

What's different from V1

From admin page → governance operations center

The cockpit identity. The page now answers "what governance risk needs attention now?" by default — risk-sorted rows, criticality chips, escalation timers (No owner · 34d), and an At-risk filter pill at the top of the bar.

Different roles per layer. Workspace gets two chips (Owner + Primary contact). Model gets three (Owner + SME + Steward). No forced uniformity.

Cross-layer insight. Each workspace row carries a Model coverage X of Y chip — the operational view tells you whether the workspace is run, the chip tells you whether its semantic accountability holds up. Click to drill into the Models tab pre-filtered.

Ownership health score (top KPI) — single number with a 60-day trend. Turns the page into a governance maturity story, not just a list.

No inheritance, no overrides. Tagging is direct. Bulk "tag all in workspace" exists as a write shortcut, not a relationship.
