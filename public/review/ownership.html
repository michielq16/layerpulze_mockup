Ownership & Stewardship — in plain language

Who is accountable for each model — the human names a document needs and an auditor asks for. Fabric tracks access; it doesn't track accountability. This is where we capture it.

📄 For: Product owner review⏱ Read: ~4 min
The one-sentence version

What we're building

Fabric can tell you who has permission to a model. It cannot tell you who is responsible for it — who signs off, who answers the auditor, who knows the business meaning.

Ownership is the LP screen where a human captures that accountability once. It then flows onto the model page and into the sign-off block of every generated document.

Four roles

Who's on the hook

Owner

Accountable

The single name responsible for the model. Signs off on documents.

SME

Knows the meaning

The subject-matter expert who can explain what the numbers mean.

Stewards

Maintain it

One or more people who keep the model healthy day to day.

Domain

Classifies it

The business area (Finance, Sales…) the model belongs to.

How it works — deliberately simple

Just tag who's accountable

Tag an item with someone→ Covered ✓· Not tagged = a gap, shown in the dots

No inheritance, no overrides, no model/report exception rules — that complexity isn't worth it. Tag a model's Owner / SME / Steward and you're done. A "tag all models in a workspace" shortcut handles bulk so big workspaces aren't tedious — it just writes the tags, it's not a relationship to maintain.

See the gaps at a glance

Coverage dots

Per workspace, one line per role — a dot for every model, filled when that role is tagged. The color tells you the health at a glance.

All models tagged About half tagged Only a few tagged Untagged (a gap)

Example — Owner ●●●●●● (all green), SME ●●●○○ (amber), Steward ●●○○○ (red). The dots are the count; no fractions, no letter codes.

Why it matters for documents

It powers the sign-off block

When you generate a document, the cover credits the Owner and Domain, and the back page renders a real sign-off block with the Owner + Stewards. No ownership captured → the document shows an explicit "No owner assigned — add via /ownership," never a fabricated name.
