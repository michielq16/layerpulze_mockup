The method · repeatable

How we rank value

Every candidate scores on four value dimensions (from the 5 fight-for-value gates + the product vision). Build-readiness is a separate axis — it sets the horizon, not the value. A high-value screen with no data yet is "held," not "low."

DimensionThe question
DifferentiatorUnique vs Microsoft? (portfolio-wide · continuously-updated · auditor-ready)
Decision densityHow big / frequent is the decision it drives? Is there a € attached?
JOIN advantageDoes it need a database join no other tool has?
Persona fitDoes it move the primary persona's KPI — the partner managing 10–50 tenants?
value = (differentiator × decision × join × persona) · horizon = value ÷ build-readiness

The discipline

What we're deliberately NOT building yet

Ten very-high-value screens beat twenty mediocre ones. The cut is as important as the list:

  • Lineage Explorer & Reports & Apps — genuinely high value, but designing them now means guessing at data that isn't ingested yet. Held, not dropped.
  • Agentic sidebar — needs the inventory wide first. Premature by design.
  • Model Health — a polish tab, not a differentiator. Doesn't earn a hero slot.
  • Standalone Tenant Activity — real value, but it's an enabler folded into Users, not its own headline.
