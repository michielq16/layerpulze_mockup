The one-sentence version

What we're building

Documenting a Power BI model by hand takes hours — and it's out of date the moment the model changes.

LayerPulse generates the whole document in seconds. We already collect everything technical from Fabric automatically. Your team adds the business knowledge once. We combine both into a single, audience-ready Word document.

How it works

Two kinds of knowledge, combined

Every document is built from two sources. One we gather for you. One your team owns.

Automatic — we collect it

Pulled straight from Microsoft Fabric. Zero effort from your team.

  • ›Tables, columns, relationships, data types
  • ›Measures (DAX) + how data is loaded (Power Query / M)
  • ›Where data comes from and where it goes (lineage)
  • ›Security: row-level + column-level + who can access
  • ›Model health score & refresh history
  • ›Usage: how many people read the downstream reports
  • ›Cost: what this model costs to run

Manual — your team adds it

The business meaning Fabric can't know. Captured once in LayerPulse, reused everywhere.

  • ›Who owns the model (Owner, expert, stewards)
  • ›What each metric/KPI means (business glossary)
  • ›Which business processes the model supports
  • ›A short note from the owner
  • ›Sign-off & "certified" stamp for trust
The simple rule: the automatic half makes the document complete. The manual half makes it trustworthy. If a measure has no business definition, we leave it blank on purpose — that empty space is the nudge to add one.
One document, four readers

The same model, written for who's reading

You generate once. LayerPulse shapes it for the reader — different sections, different depth, different tone.

Auditor

Proof for compliance

For SOC 2 / audit evidence

Security rules, who can access what, refresh reliability, findings, and a real sign-off block with the owner + stewards.

Analyst

Onboarding handoff

For someone new to the model

Plain-English measures, the full glossary, who to ask, and how fresh the data is — everything to get productive fast.

Executive

The one-page summary

For the QBR / leadership

Big KPIs with definitions, reach (how many people use it), cost to run, and the owner's note. No technical detail.

Engineer

The full technical manual

For whoever maintains it

Everything — schema, DAX, Power Query, storage, hierarchies, security, dormant measures, lineage. The complete picture.

Over to you

A few decisions we need from you

Nothing urgent — these shape the build. Plain choices, no homework.

  • 1Whose logo on the cover?For audit docs about a customer — your partner logo, or the customer's? (Likely the customer's. May need a legal check.)
  • 2How long do we keep generated documents?Auditors often need 7 years; storage grows fast. 90 days / 1 year / forever?
  • 3Is the Business Glossary its own thing, or part of Documents?It powers the document definitions, so it's a dependency either way — but it could be a standalone governance screen.
  • 4"Ownership" means two things.Fabric already tracks access ownership (permissions). What we added is business ownership (who's accountable). Keep them as separate concepts? (We think yes.)
